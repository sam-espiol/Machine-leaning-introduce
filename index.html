From Theory to Breakthroughs

Explore the evolution of Machine Learning—from foundational algorithms to modern-day innovations driving AI today.

Development History

1950s: Birth of ML

Alan Turing proposes the idea of learning machines. Arthur Samuel coins the term "Machine Learning" in 1959.

1980s: Neural Networks Resurge

Backpropagation algorithm popularized, reigniting research in multi-layer perceptrons and early deep networks.

1997: Deep Blue Victory

IBM's Deep Blue defeats world chess champion Garry Kasparov, showcasing computational power in game-playing AI.

2012: Deep Learning Breakthrough

AlexNet wins ImageNet challenge by a large margin, accelerating deep convolutional network research.

2018–Present: Transformer Era

"Attention Is All You Need" introduces transformers, revolutionizing NLP and enabling large language models.

Key Achievements

1998

Convolutional Neural Networks

Yann LeCun demonstrates CNNs for digit recognition, paving the way for modern image-based AI.

2016

AlphaGo

DeepMind's AlphaGo defeats Go champion Lee Sedol, illustrating RL's potential in complex strategy games.

2020

GPT-3 Release

OpenAI's GPT-3 showcases unprecedented language generation capabilities, highlighting transformer scalability.
